=== Exercise 1.8 — Certificate trust chain debugging

[TIP]
.Zero Trust for machines, not only people
====
Users authenticate to IdM; **services** must also prove they belong to the organisation. A self-signed HTTPS cert says “trust me because I say so”—there is **no anchor** tying the service to your enterprise PKI. ZTA expects **verifiable service identity** (here, via the IdM CA) so automation and humans do not train themselves to click through certificate warnings—a behaviour attackers exploit.
====

==== Scenario

A test HTTPS endpoint on central uses a **self-signed** certificate instead of the IdM CA. Clients that trust only IdM will fail TLS verification.

*Instructor:* `ansible-playbook section1/playbooks/break-cert-trust.yml`

==== Steps

. Observe failure:
+
[source,bash]
----
curl https://central.zta.lab:9443/
----
+
Expect certificate verification error.

. Inspect presented cert:
+
[source,bash]
----
echo | openssl s_client -connect central.zta.lab:9443 2>/dev/null | \
  openssl x509 -text -noout | grep -E "Issuer|Subject|Not "
----

. Compare with IdM UI cert (port 443).

. On central, request IdM-signed cert with `ipa-getcert` (see `section1/README.md` for full `ipa-getcert request` example and paths).

. Point the test service at IdM-signed cert/key; restart service; verify `curl` succeeds and issuer is IdM CA.

==== Validation

* [ ] Initial `curl` fails (untrusted issuer)
* [ ] `openssl` shows self-signed issuer on :9443
* [ ] After fix, `curl` succeeds and issuer is IdM CA
